- basic units + measurements (and their alternate titles!)
	- distance, weight/mass, liquid/solid volume, temp, heat

- basic SI prefixes

- dealing with fractions (people hate fractions)
	- fractions are just division ($\frac{2}{5} = 2 \div 5$)
	- $x = \frac{x}{1}$
	- $\frac{2}{5} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{6}{20}$ (multiplying straight across)
		- note: simplifying fractions technically isn't necessary
	- $\frac{x}{y} = \frac{x}{1} \div \frac{y}{1} = \frac{x}{1} \times \frac{1}{y}$ (keep, change, flip)

- scientific notation: how to determine it
	- number bigger than 1 -> exponent of 10 will be positive
	- number smaller than 1 -> exponent of 10 will be negative
	- also: how to find it on your calculator?

- significant figures
	- why do we use them?
		- answer -> significant figures tell us how precise our measuring tools were
			- "we don't want to get MORE precise the further along the equation we go, we want to remain equally precise"
	- they're a little difficult to get a grasp on
	- determining number of sigfigs
	- "how many sigfigs should my answer have after completing a question?"/rounding answers
	- addition/subtraction rules, too

- logarithms (base-10)
	- how to use them and undo them
		- $10^{\log (x)} = x$
	- why they're important to this course
		- pH, equilibrium, first-order int. rate laws, $\Delta\text{E} = \Delta\text{E}^{\circ} - \frac{\text{RT}}{\text{nF}}\ln \text{K}$, $\Delta\text{G} = -\text{RT} \ln \text{K}$ 

- percentages in decimal form ($90\% = 0.90$)
	- good for mass spectroscopy

- working with equations and formulas
	- review how to isolate variables + rearrange formulas
		- add/sub, mult/div, $x^y$/$\sqrt[y]{x}$
		- seven layer bean dip/multi-step isolations
	- also, learn how to recognize relationships between variables in formulas
